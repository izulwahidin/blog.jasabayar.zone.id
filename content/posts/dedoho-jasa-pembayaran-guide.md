## Your Top Questions About Dedoho Payment Service Answered

Understanding the financial mechanics of your payment gateway—specifically fees and payout timing—is crucial for effective cash flow management and compliance. Here, we address the most common inquiries merchants have about Dedoho.

### Q1. What is the typical transaction fee structure for Dedoho?

The fee structure for any payment gateway, including Dedoho, is typically based on a **per-transaction fee model**, meaning a small charge is applied every time a customer successfully completes a payment. This model ensures that you only pay when you generate revenue. However, to maintain high-level expertise, it's essential to understand that this fee is **variable** based on the payment method your customer chooses.

For instance, payments processed via international credit cards will inherently carry a higher cost than those processed through local bank transfers (Virtual Accounts) or domestic e-wallets, reflecting the underlying interchange and network fees. As a general industry guideline observed by financial analysts in the region, card-based transactions often incur a percentage-plus-fixed-fee, while Virtual Accounts and local bank transfers are frequently charged a flat fixed fee per transaction. Businesses should consult the official Dedoho Merchant Agreement to obtain the precise, tiered fee schedule that aligns with their transaction volume, as high-volume merchants often qualify for custom, discounted rates.

### Q2. How long does the fund settlement (payout) process take with Dedoho?

Fund settlement, or the time it takes for the collected revenue to be deposited into your designated merchant bank account, is a primary concern for business liquidity. Dedoho typically operates on an accelerated settlement cycle, which is generally **T+1 or T+2**.

* **T+1 (Transaction Date plus One Business Day):** This means the funds from all transactions processed on Monday (T) will be settled into your bank account on Tuesday (+1). This is often the standard for high-volume or preferred merchants who have an established history of reliable and low-risk transactions, demonstrating the platform’s commitment to rapid capital access.
* **T+2 (Transaction Date plus Two Business Days):** This standard cycle is common across the payment industry and may apply to new merchants, international transactions, or those using certain high-risk payment methods.

It is paramount for reliable financial planning that merchants review their specific **Service Level Agreement (SLA)** with Dedoho. While most digital payments like e-wallets and local bank transfers facilitate quicker settlement (often T+1), card payments may occasionally adhere to the T+2 standard due to the rigorous clearing and fraud verification processes mandated by global card networks. A clear understanding of your settlement cycle, which you can verify within your Dedoho Merchant Dashboard, is a key component of operational transparency.
