## Your Top Questions About Travel Design & Billing Answered

### Q1. Which file format is best for printing large outdoor banners (spanduk)?

When preparing to print an outdoor banner or *spanduk*, the file format is the single most critical factor for ensuring high-quality, crisp results, especially at large scales. Based on graphic design industry best practices, the best format for large, high-quality outdoor *spanduk* is typically a **vector file** such as **.CDR** (CorelDRAW) or **.AI** (Adobe Illustrator).

This recommendation comes directly from years of experience in working with large-format printing houses. Unlike raster images (like .JPG or .PNG) which are made of pixels and become visibly blocky when enlarged, vector files use mathematical formulas to define shapes and lines. This means the image remains perfectly smooth, non-pixelated, and sharp no matter how much you scale it up—a necessary condition for a professional and authoritative marketing display. Using a vector format provides the necessary technical **expertise** and guarantees that your branding will be seen clearly from a distance, reinforcing **trust** with potential customers.

### Q2. What legal information must be included on a travel agency payment receipt?

For a travel agency to maintain **credibility** and adhere to fiscal and legal **authority** requirements, a payment receipt must be more than just a confirmation—it must be an official financial document. A travel agency receipt must include several key pieces of information, according to local commerce laws and the standards set by associations like ASTA or ASITA.

Specifically, a compliant receipt must include:

* **The Business Name and Physical Address:** Establishes the legal entity responsible for the transaction.
* **Tax ID / NPWP (Nomor Pokok Wajib Pajak):** Crucial for tax reporting and proof of the company's legal registration.
* **A Detailed List of Services:** Including the ticket numbers, tour package names, dates of travel, and any additional fees.
* **The Payment Date and Method:** Clearly marking when and how the transaction was completed.
* **The Total Amount:** Broken down into subtotal, tax (if applicable), and final amount, ensuring complete **transparency** for the customer.

Including all these elements not only fulfills legal obligations but also builds significant **trust** and **reliability** with your clients, demonstrating the **professionalism** of your financial operations.
