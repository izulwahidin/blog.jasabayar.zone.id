Once these documents are uploaded, Skrill's compliance team will review them. While the process can sometimes be completed in a few hours, verification typically takes up to 24 hours. ## Sending Money Internationally: Skrill's Transfer Rates vs. Competitors

### How Skrill's Foreign Exchange (FX) Markup Affects Transfer Costs

When sending money internationally via Skrill, the transfer fee itself is often low or even 0% depending on the destination and funding method. However, the **true cost** of an international money transfer is generally found in the **foreign exchange (FX) rate markup**. This markup is the difference between the rate Skrill offers you and the 'interbank' or 'mid-market' rate—the rate banks use to trade with each other. For the average user, this hidden cost, rather than the explicit transfer fee, represents the most significant expense. Understanding this markup is crucial for managing your total transfer expenditure.

To demonstrate this point, let's look at a hypothetical transfer of EUR 1,000 to USD. As of our last review of major payment providers, Skrill’s exchange rate for this corridor included a margin that typically results in the recipient receiving **less USD** compared to a mid-market rate specialist like Wise. For instance, while Skrill might charge a very low visible fee, a specialist service leveraging transparent rates might deliver \$1,080, whereas Skrill might deliver \$1,065 for the same EUR 1,000. This disparity highlights why evaluating the final amount received, not just the initial fee, is a hallmark of **transparency and accountability** in a financial service. The exchange rate markup is the essential detail that determines the "real" cost difference.

To maximize your savings, it is always recommended to **check the offered exchange rate** before you finalize the transfer. Unlike a floating rate, Skrill's quoted rate is *locked in* the moment you confirm the transaction. This guarantees the recipient receives the exact amount shown, but it also means you must manually check and compare that rate against the interbank rate (easily found via a simple search) before you commit to ensure you are getting the best value.

### Step-by-Step: Sending Funds to a Bank Account Overseas

The process for sending funds internationally from your Skrill wallet to an overseas bank account is straightforward and highly secure:

1.  **Log in to your Skrill account** and navigate to the **"Send Money"** section.
2.  **Select "Send to Bank Account."**
3.  **Enter the recipient's bank details,** including the bank name, IBAN (for Europe) or account number, and the SWIFT/BIC code.
4.  **Input the amount** you wish to send and select the currency. Skrill will instantly display the final, **all-inclusive exchange rate** and the exact amount the recipient will receive in their local currency.
5.  **Review the summary.** This is the moment to compare the rate against competitors. If you are satisfied with the final amount, click "Confirm" to lock in the rate and complete the transfer.
6.  **Confirm the transaction** using two-factor authentication (2FA). The funds are then processed and sent for delivery, typically taking between 2 and 5 business days to clear into the recipient's account.
